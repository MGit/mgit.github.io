Martin Guay

I lead the research and development of innovative technologies at the intersection of artificial intelligence, graphics, and human-computer interaction. In 2018, I established marker-less motion capture capabilities and in-camera VFX called synth2track, which is operated today by Industrial Light & Magic (ILM). More recently, I established genAI for 3D motion for fast motion authoring and editing; both at Pixar and ILM. Prior to this, I pioneered generative conversation models for 3D AI avatars and chat bots, that understand and speak with people.

During my Ph.D. I mathematically ellucidated artistic principles that yield clear visual communication and leveragged optimization to design new interfaces for automatically authoring compelling 3D movements. Physics simulation increase the immersive experience of 3D content and I conceived new approximations of the Navier-stokes equations that simplify the simulation of 3D fluids; rendering fire and water effects more accessible to a greater audience. Then I created fully simulated characters that---similar to robots in the real world---react in a unique and realistic way to every interaction.

During my career, I had the privilege of experiencing both long term research and short term production. I started my journey in production developping and optimizing video games on various platforms.
